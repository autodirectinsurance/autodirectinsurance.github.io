Cheap Non-Owner Car Insurance Without a Credit Check

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Finding affordable car insurance when you don’t own a vehicle can be tricky—especially if you’re also dealing with credit challenges. Many traditional insurers rely heavily on credit scores to determine premiums, leaving those with poor or no credit history at a disadvantage. Fortunately, cheap non-owner car insurance without a credit check is possible if you know where to look.

Why Non-Owner Car Insurance Matters

Non-owner car insurance is designed for people who frequently drive but don’t own a car. Whether you rent vehicles often, borrow friends’ cars, or use car-sharing services, this type of policy provides liability coverage in case of an accident.

Who Needs Non-Owner Car Insurance?

  • Frequent renters – If you rely on rental cars for business or travel, a non-owner policy can save you from expensive rental insurance add-ons.
  • Rideshare drivers – Some gig platforms require non-owner policies if you don’t have a personal auto policy.
  • People with suspended licenses – In some states, you may need an SR-22 filing to reinstate your license, and non-owner insurance can help.
  • Urban dwellers – If you live in a city with strong public transit but occasionally drive, this coverage keeps you protected.

The Problem with Credit Checks in Insurance

Most insurers use credit-based insurance scores to assess risk and set premiums. Studies show that people with lower credit scores tend to file more claims, leading insurers to charge them higher rates. However, this practice has faced criticism for disproportionately affecting low-income individuals and communities of color.

States That Ban Credit-Based Insurance Scores

A few states have taken steps to limit or ban the use of credit in insurance pricing:
- California – Prohibits credit checks for auto insurance.
- Massachusetts – Does not allow credit history to influence premiums.
- Hawaii – Restricts the use of credit in insurance decisions.

If you live in one of these states, you may have an easier time finding non-owner car insurance without a credit check.

How to Find Cheap Non-Owner Car Insurance Without a Credit Check

1. Look for Insurers That Don’t Use Credit Scores

Some companies specialize in high-risk or no-credit-check insurance. These include:
- The General – Known for affordable policies for drivers with past issues.
- Dairyland – Offers non-owner policies without strict credit requirements.
- SafeAuto – Provides basic liability coverage at competitive rates.

2. Compare Quotes from Multiple Providers

Rates can vary significantly between insurers, so shopping around is crucial. Use online comparison tools or work with an independent agent who can access multiple carriers.

3. Opt for Minimum Liability Coverage

If you’re on a tight budget, sticking to state-mandated minimum liability coverage can keep costs low. Just be aware that this may leave you underinsured in a serious accident.

4. Ask About Discounts

Even without a credit check, you may qualify for discounts such as:
- Safe driver discounts – If you have a clean driving record.
- Paid-in-full discounts – Paying your premium upfront instead of monthly.
- Bundling discounts – Combining with renters or other insurance policies.

5. Consider Usage-Based Insurance

Some insurers offer pay-per-mile or usage-based policies, which can be cheaper if you drive infrequently.

The Future of Credit Checks in Insurance

With growing scrutiny over fairness in insurance pricing, more states may follow California and Massachusetts in banning credit-based insurance scoring. Advocacy groups argue that financial history shouldn’t dictate insurance costs, especially when driving behavior is a more accurate risk indicator.

In the meantime, drivers seeking non-owner policies without credit checks have options—it just takes a bit of research and persistence. By focusing on insurers that prioritize driving records over credit history, you can find affordable coverage that meets your needs.

Remember, the cheapest policy isn’t always the best. Make sure you have enough coverage to protect yourself financially in case of an accident.
