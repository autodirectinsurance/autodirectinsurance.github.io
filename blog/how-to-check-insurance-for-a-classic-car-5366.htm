How to Check Insurance for a Classic Car

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Classic cars are more than just vehicles—they’re pieces of history, symbols of craftsmanship, and often cherished investments. However, insuring a classic car isn’t the same as insuring a modern daily driver. With rising global concerns like climate change, supply chain disruptions, and fluctuating market values, securing the right insurance for your classic car requires careful consideration. Here’s a detailed guide to help you navigate the process.

Why Classic Car Insurance Is Different

Unique Valuation Challenges

Unlike modern cars, classic cars don’t depreciate in the same way. Their value often appreciates, especially if they’re rare or well-maintained. Standard auto insurance policies typically cover actual cash value (ACV), which accounts for depreciation. For classic cars, you’ll want an agreed value policy, where you and the insurer agree on the car’s worth upfront.

Usage Restrictions

Classic car insurance usually comes with mileage limits. Insurers assume you’re not driving your 1967 Mustang to work every day. Exceeding these limits could void your coverage. Some policies also restrict how you use the car—for example, no ridesharing or commercial use.

Storage Requirements

Many insurers require classic cars to be stored in a secure, climate-controlled garage. With increasing incidents of extreme weather due to climate change, proper storage isn’t just a policy requirement—it’s a necessity to protect your investment.

Steps to Check Insurance for Your Classic Car

1. Research Specialty Insurers

Not all insurance companies offer classic car coverage. Look for providers specializing in vintage and collector vehicles, such as Hagerty, Grundy, or American Collectors Insurance. These companies understand the unique needs of classic car owners.

2. Determine the Car’s Value

Before getting a quote, you’ll need to know your car’s value. Options include:
- Professional Appraisal: Hire a certified appraiser familiar with classic cars.
- Auction Records: Check recent sales of similar models.
- Online Tools: Use valuation guides like Kelley Blue Book’s classic car section or NADAguides.

3. Compare Coverage Options

Classic car insurance policies vary. Key features to compare:
- Agreed Value vs. Stated Value: Agreed value is preferable—it guarantees the payout amount in case of a total loss.
- Roadside Assistance: Many classic cars aren’t as reliable as modern ones.
- Spare Parts Coverage: Some policies cover the cost of rare or custom parts.

4. Check for Discounts

Insurers often offer discounts for:
- Club Memberships: Being part of a classic car club.
- Multi-Car Policies: Insuring multiple classics under one policy.
- Safe Driving Records: A clean history can lower premiums.

5. Review Policy Exclusions

Read the fine print. Common exclusions include:
- Racing or Track Use: Most policies won’t cover competitive events.
- Unapproved Modifications: Altering the car from its original specs may void coverage.
- International Travel: If you plan to drive your classic abroad, check if the policy covers it.

Emerging Trends Affecting Classic Car Insurance

Climate Change and Natural Disasters

With hurricanes, floods, and wildfires becoming more frequent, classic car owners must consider geographic risks. If you live in a high-risk area, look for policies that include natural disaster coverage.

Supply Chain Issues

The global shortage of parts—especially for rare classics—means repairs can take longer and cost more. Ensure your policy accounts for these delays and inflated costs.

Electrification and Modifications

Some owners are converting classics to electric vehicles (EVs). While eco-friendly, these modifications can complicate insurance. Discuss any major changes with your insurer to avoid coverage gaps.

Final Tips for Classic Car Owners

  • Document Everything: Keep photos, receipts, and maintenance records. This helps with claims and proving value.
  • Drive Safely: Classic cars lack modern safety features. Take extra caution on the road.
  • Reassess Annually: Market values change. Review your policy yearly to ensure adequate coverage.

By following these steps, you can protect your classic car and enjoy it for years to come. Whether you’re cruising in a ’57 Chevy or a vintage Porsche, the right insurance ensures peace of mind in an unpredictable world.
