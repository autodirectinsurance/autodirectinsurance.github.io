Check Insurance for a Home Solar Panel System

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Solar energy is no longer a futuristic concept—it’s here, and it’s transforming how homeowners power their lives. With rising electricity costs and growing environmental concerns, more people are investing in home solar panel systems. But while the benefits are clear, many overlook a critical step: checking their insurance coverage.

Why Insurance Matters for Solar Panel Systems

Installing solar panels is a significant investment, often costing tens of thousands of dollars. Protecting that investment should be a top priority. Here’s why insurance is essential:

1. Protection Against Physical Damage

Solar panels are exposed to the elements—hail, storms, falling branches, and even wildlife can cause damage. Without proper coverage, repairs or replacements come out of your pocket.

2. Liability Concerns

If your solar panels malfunction and cause damage to your property or a neighbor’s, liability coverage can save you from costly lawsuits.

3. Theft and Vandalism

While solar panel theft is rare, it does happen. Insurance ensures you’re not left footing the bill if your system is stolen or vandalized.

Does Homeowners Insurance Cover Solar Panels?

The short answer: it depends. Most standard homeowners insurance policies do cover solar panels, but there are caveats.

Coverage Under Existing Policies

  • Dwelling Coverage: If your panels are permanently attached to your roof, they’re often considered part of your home and covered under dwelling insurance.
  • Personal Property Coverage: Ground-mounted systems may fall under personal property coverage, which has different limits.

Potential Gaps in Coverage

  • System Value vs. Policy Limits: Some policies may not fully cover high-value solar installations.
  • Deductibles: A high deductible could make small claims impractical.
  • Exclusions: Some policies exclude certain perils (e.g., floods or earthquakes).

How to Ensure Your Solar Panels Are Fully Protected

1. Review Your Current Policy

Before installing solar panels, contact your insurance provider to confirm coverage. Ask:
- Are solar panels included under dwelling or personal property coverage?
- Are there any exclusions (e.g., wind or hail damage)?
- Do I need to increase my coverage limits?

2. Consider a Separate Solar Insurance Policy

Some insurers offer specialized solar panel insurance. These policies may provide:
- Higher coverage limits
- Lower deductibles
- Protection against business interruptions (if you sell excess energy back to the grid)

3. Look Into Warranty Extensions

Many solar manufacturers offer warranties, but they often exclude certain types of damage. An extended warranty or equipment protection plan can fill those gaps.

The Financial Impact of Solar Panel Insurance

Adding solar panels to your home can affect your insurance premiums, but the increase is usually minimal (around $10–$50 per year). The trade-off? Peace of mind knowing your investment is secure.

Will My Premiums Go Up?

  • Yes, but not drastically: Since solar panels increase your home’s value, insurers may adjust premiums slightly.
  • Discounts may apply: Some insurers offer green energy discounts for solar-powered homes.

Real-World Scenarios: When Insurance Saved Homeowners

Case 1: Storm Damage

A homeowner in Texas had their solar panels damaged by a hailstorm. Their insurance covered the full replacement cost—saving them over $15,000.

Case 2: Electrical Fire

A faulty inverter caused a small fire in a California home. The homeowner’s policy covered both the fire damage and the cost of a new inverter.

Final Tips Before You Go Solar

  • Document everything: Keep receipts, warranties, and installation records.
  • Work with a reputable installer: Proper installation reduces risks and insurance complications.
  • Update your policy immediately: Don’t wait until after installation—adjust coverage beforehand.

Solar panels are a smart, sustainable choice, but they’re also a major financial commitment. Taking the time to check your insurance ensures that your green energy solution doesn’t turn into an unexpected expense.
