How to Get 2 Wheeler Insurance for a Bike with a Previous Claim History

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Getting two-wheeler insurance for a bike with a previous claim history can be tricky, but it’s not impossible. Whether you’ve filed a claim due to an accident, theft, or damage, insurers may view you as a higher-risk customer. However, with the right approach, you can still secure affordable and comprehensive coverage.

Understanding How Claim History Affects Your Insurance

Why Insurers Care About Past Claims

Insurance companies assess risk before offering coverage. A history of claims suggests that you might be more likely to file another one in the future. This makes insurers cautious, leading to:
- Higher premiums – You may pay more than someone with a clean record.
- Stricter terms – Some insurers may exclude certain types of coverage.
- Limited options – Not all providers may be willing to insure you.

Types of Claims That Impact Your Policy

Not all claims affect your insurance the same way. Here’s how different claims may influence your policy:
- At-fault accidents – These have the biggest impact, as they suggest risky riding behavior.
- Theft claims – If your bike was stolen, insurers may see you as a target for future thefts.
- Minor damage claims – Small claims (like scratches) may have less impact than major ones.

Steps to Get Insurance After a Previous Claim

1. Shop Around for the Best Deals

Not all insurers treat claim history the same. Some specialize in high-risk policies, while others may offer better rates if your claim was minor.
- Compare quotes from at least 3-4 insurers.
- Check online aggregators – Websites like Policybazaar (India) or Compare.com (U.S.) can help.
- Look for specialized insurers – Some companies focus on riders with past claims.

2. Consider a Higher Deductible

A higher deductible (the amount you pay before insurance kicks in) can lower your premium.
- Pros: Lower monthly costs.
- Cons: You pay more out of pocket if you file another claim.

3. Improve Your Riding Record

If possible, avoid filing claims for minor damages. Over time, a clean record can help reduce your premiums.
- Take a defensive riding course – Some insurers offer discounts for certified riders.
- Follow traffic rules – Fewer violations mean fewer red flags for insurers.

4. Explore No-Claim Bonus (NCB) Protection

Some policies allow you to protect your No-Claim Bonus even after a claim.
- How it works: You pay a small extra fee to retain your NCB after one claim.
- Best for: Riders who rarely file claims but had one recent incident.

5. Opt for Third-Party Insurance (If Full Coverage Is Too Expensive)

If comprehensive insurance is too costly, third-party liability coverage is the legal minimum in many countries.
- Pros: Cheaper, meets legal requirements.
- Cons: Doesn’t cover your own bike’s damages.

How Technology Is Changing Bike Insurance

Usage-Based Insurance (UBI)

Some insurers now offer policies based on how you ride, tracked via telematics or smartphone apps.
- Pay-as-you-ride: Premiums adjust based on mileage and riding habits.
- Safer riders pay less – If your data shows cautious riding, you could get discounts.

AI and Fraud Detection

Insurers use AI to detect fraudulent claims, which helps keep premiums lower for honest riders.
- Faster processing – AI speeds up claim approvals.
- Reduced fraud – Fewer false claims mean better rates for everyone.

The Impact of Climate Change on Bike Insurance

Rising Premiums Due to Extreme Weather

With increasing floods, storms, and wildfires, insurers are adjusting rates.
- Flood-prone areas may see higher premiums.
- Theft risks – Climate migration can lead to higher crime rates in some regions.

Eco-Friendly Discounts

Some insurers reward riders of electric bikes (e-bikes) with lower premiums.
- Government incentives – In some countries, e-bike policies are subsidized.
- Lower risk – E-bikes may have fewer accident claims than high-speed petrol bikes.

Legal Considerations When Insuring a Bike with Past Claims

Disclosure Rules

Hiding past claims can lead to policy cancellation or claim rejection.
- Always be honest – Insurers can verify your claim history.
- Check local laws – Some regions have strict disclosure requirements.

Time Limits on Claim Impact

Most insurers only look at claims from the last 3-5 years.
- Older claims matter less – If your last claim was long ago, you may get better rates now.

Final Tips for Affordable Coverage

  • Bundle policies – Some insurers offer discounts if you buy bike + car or home insurance together.
  • Loyalty discounts – Staying with the same insurer for years can reduce costs.
  • Negotiate – If you have a good record otherwise, ask for a better deal.

By following these steps, you can still get reliable two-wheeler insurance even with a previous claim. The key is to research, compare, and choose the best option for your needs.
